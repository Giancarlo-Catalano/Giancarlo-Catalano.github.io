Mining Potentially Explanatory Patterns via Partial Solutions

About the Methodology

This toolkit provides a transparent Explainable AI (XAI) methodology for combinatorial optimization problems solved by black-box metaheuristics. The goal is to explain the fitness of solutions by finding patterns—called Partial Solutions (PSs)—that are associated with high fitness (e.g., the pattern * * 1 1 1).

  • Data Collection: We run a metaheuristic and store all evaluated solutions, their fitness values, and optional "proxy-fitness" functions into a Reference Population (PRef).
  • Linkage Learning: We analyze the PRef to find dependencies between variables, creating a linkage table.
  • Multi-Objective Mining: We use an NSGA-II algorithm with custom operators to find a Pareto front of Partial Solutions that optimize distinct criteria: simplicity, fitness quality, atomicity, and robustness.
  • Evaluation & Descriptors: The discovered PSs are sorted using Minimax Regret to find the best all-around patterns. We then highlight "descriptors"—proxy-fitness values that are statistically extreme for that specific pattern.

Generate the Reference Population (PRef)

This tool generates the base dataset of evaluated solutions required for all subsequent steps.

  • Select a benchmark problem (Royal Road, Trap-K, or Graph Colouring).
  • Configure your preferred metaheuristic (Random Search or a μ + λ Genetic Algorithm).
  • Set your evaluation budget and generate the data.
  • Download: Save the resulting PRef.csv. Optionally, calculate and download the Proxy_Fitness.csv.

Learn Variable Linkage

Before mining patterns, we must determine how the problem's variables interact with one another. This allows us to score the "Atomicity" and "Independence" of future Partial Solutions.

  • Upload your generated PRef.csv file.
  • Select a linkage learning method: Dominance-based (Cramer's V) or Mutual Information of Survival.
  • Download: Save the computed Linkage_Table.csv.

Mine Explanatory Patterns

The core NSGA-II mining engine. This tool ties all your data together to discover and evaluate the best Partial Solutions.

  • Upload your context files: PRef.csv, Proxy_Fitness.csv, and Linkage_Table.csv.
  • Choose to perform an Any-Pattern (Global) search or a Subset-of-Solution (Local) search on a specific high-fitness target.
  • Toggle the specific objectives you wish to minimize (e.g., Mean Fitness, Star Count, Atomicity).
  • Run the NSGA-II algorithm to discover patterns, view their Minimax Regret rankings, and calculate their extreme ECDF descriptors.
